Install

OpenScreenShot is available on the Chrome Web Store.

  1. Open the OpenScreenShot listing in Chrome.
  2. Click Add to Chrome.
  3. Accept the permissions. The extension needs access to the active tab and the ability to run a content script to capture the page.
  4. The OpenScreenShot icon appears in your toolbar. Click it to open the popup.

Capture a screenshot

Click the OpenScreenShot icon in your toolbar. Choose one of the three capture modes. The screenshot is processed locally in your browser and then opens in the annotation editor.

Capture modes

Full Page

Captures the entire scrolling page and stitches it into one image. The extension scrolls the page viewport by viewport, captures each tile, and composites them together. Fixed headers are captured on the first tile and composited once at the top.

Visible Area

Captures the current viewport instantly. No scrolling. Use this for a quick screenshot of what is on screen right now.

Selected Region

Click and drag to draw a rectangle. Resize handles appear on each edge and corner. Use the arrow keys to nudge the selection by one pixel at a time, or drag the handles to resize. Press Enter to confirm or Esc to cancel.

Annotate

After every capture, the annotation editor opens in a new tab. The editor loads the most recent capture from local storage.

  • Rectangle — draw a border rectangle.
  • Arrow — draw a directional arrow.
  • Pen — freehand drawing.
  • Text — add a text box. Use the font-size slider to adjust size.
  • Blur — pixelate a region to hide sensitive information.
  • Crop — trim the image to a selected area.

Style controls include an 8-color palette, three stroke widths, and a font-size slider. Your choices are remembered across sessions. Undo and redo are available with Ctrl+Z / Ctrl+Shift+Z (or ⌘Z / ⌘Shift+Z on macOS).

Export

Click the Export button in the top bar of the editor. Choose a format:

  • PNG — lossless, supports transparency.
  • JPEG — smaller file size, with adjustable quality.
  • WebP — modern format, good balance of size and quality.
  • PDF — export as a single image page, fit to A4/Letter, or split across multiple pages with a 5mm overlap so content is not cut mid-line.

Privacy

OpenScreenShot processes everything locally in your browser. Captures are stored in Chrome local storage and are not uploaded to any server. The extension has no analytics, no telemetry, and no third-party services.

See the Privacy Policy for the full details.
